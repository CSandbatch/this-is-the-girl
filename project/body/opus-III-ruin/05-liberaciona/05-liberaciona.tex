\PoemHead{Liberación(a)}{}

\begin{stanza}
We met where the border drips\\
\quad sweat into the armpit river.\\
Wild María with sulphur eyes,\\
\quad calico skirt \& belt of twine\\
\quad laughing like a bullfighter banshee.\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
Her name in town was \textit{Liberación(a).}\\
Three knives she had \& brothers two—\\
\quad kept \textit{dos pasos más} between her love \& shoes.\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
She said, “Sleep is for the pig boys.\\
I dream in \textit{rayo, loco de sol}\\
you dream in English, snowy \& slow.”\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
She called me el extranjero\\
\quad kicking dust on my hands.\\
I said, “loyal am I!\\
For you alone my midnights burn.”\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
She spat: “\textit{¿Te quiero?} Don’t lie.\\
You’d church my body just to turn my color.”\\
\end{stanza}

\pagebreak


\begin{stanza}
María dug a hole in my stare\\
\quad deep-drilled sighing smoke.\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
“María, ¿estás desnuda?” I said,\\
\quad but she kissed her fingers red\\
\quad threw a frog in the missionary’s eye.\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
My leap-year heart given to her.\\
Her promises—ribbon slashes.\\
\end{stanza}

\begin{stanza}
“\textit{María, ¿por qué lloras?}” I said.\\
She laughed, “\textit{Porque} I can’t stop the frogs.”\\
\quad Then vanished into the \textit{arcaicos} of night.
\end{stanza}
