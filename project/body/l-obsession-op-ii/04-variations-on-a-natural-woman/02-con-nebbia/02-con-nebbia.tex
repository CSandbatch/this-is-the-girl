\begin{stanza}
Some lace on the sill radio hiss, the damp of old rooms\\
a flutter (it skips) of longing in lemon-tree blooms.\\
It is nothing, you say, just a street in the sun,\\
but your calves in the daylight (the way you run)\\
I will think of the salt on your skin, your disordered care.\\
And what would you do, seeing me stare?
\end{stanza}
